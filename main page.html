Chocolate comes from

Cocoa beans are harvested from the pods of the cacao tree.The beans are placed in wooden boxes or covered with banana leaves for fermentation, which lasts about 5-7 days.

Harvesting and Fermenting

The roasted beans are cracked open to separate the nibs from the shells.
The shells are removed, leaving behind the nibs, which are the edible part of the bean.

Cracking and Winnowing

The nibs are ground into a liquid called cocoa liquor.The liquor is further refined through a process called conching, where it's continuously mixed to develop the flavor and texture.

Grinding and Conching

The chocolate is slowly cooled and reheated to stabilize the cocoa butter crystals, giving it a shiny appearance and smooth texture. The tempered chocolate is poured into molds to set into various shapes.

Tempering and Molding

The molded chocolate is cooled to solidify.
Once cooled, the chocolate is wrapped and packaged for distribution.
